# Lesson Note - PTE Core Lesson 5

Date: 2026-06-29
Lesson: 5. Write Email: Time Control
Task Type: Write Email
Topic: Urgent request for login details

## Lesson Summary

Richard practised a PTE Core Write Email task under the Lesson 5 focus: time control.

The task required him to write a short email to a course support team because login details had not been received and the online English course would start the next morning.

## Strengths

- The email included a greeting, opening, explanation, request, thanks, and closing.
- The task content was complete.
- The tone was polite and suitable for a support request.
- The urgency was clear.

## Main Problems

- Grammar accuracy decreased under time pressure.
- Several verb patterns need reinforcement:
  - I am unable to log in to...
  - Could you please send...
  - Since the course starts tomorrow morning, this is urgent.
- Some expressions were too heavy or unnatural:
  - “I am writing to express that” should be replaced by “I am writing to let you know that”.
- There were too many paragraph breaks for a short exam email.

## Key Teaching Point

For PTE Core Write Email, Richard should choose simple and safe sentence patterns instead of trying to sound complicated.

Good simple pattern:

“I am writing to let you know that I am unable to log in to my online English course.”

## Useful Patterns

1. I am writing to let you know that I am unable to + verb.
2. Since + situation, this is quite urgent.
3. Could you please send me + item + by email as soon as possible?
