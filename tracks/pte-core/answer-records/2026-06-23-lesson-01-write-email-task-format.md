## 1. Original Question

You recently borrowed a book from your friend. Unfortunately, you lost the book.

Write an email to your friend. In your email:

- Explain what happened.
- Apologize for losing the book.
- Tell your friend how you will solve the problem.

## 2. Exam Instructions

- Write 50–120 words.
- Write in an appropriate email style.
- Include a greeting and closing.
- Use clear and simple English.

## 6. Mistake Analysis

### Content Problems

No major content problem. All task points were covered.

### Form Problems

The format is acceptable, but punctuation should be improved. Some sentences need full stops.

### Grammar Problems

- Use "I am writing this email" instead of "I wrote this letter".
- Use "on my bookshelf" instead of "in my bookshelf".
- Use "I have searched everywhere" instead of a long unnatural phrase.
- Use "Unfortunately" or "At this point" instead of "For this moment".

### Vocabulary Problems

- Use "I still cannot find it" instead of an unnatural passive expression.
- Use "give you a replacement copy" instead of "return you a new one".

## 7. Corrected Version

Hi Julia,

I hope you are doing well.

I am writing to apologize for losing the book "Introduction to Algorithms" that I borrowed from you.

I placed the book on my bookshelf, but it later went missing. I have searched everywhere in my house, but I have not been able to find it. Unfortunately, I have to admit that the book is lost.

I am very sorry for this. I will buy a new copy online and give it to you as soon as possible.

Thank you for your understanding, and I apologize for any inconvenience caused.

Kind regards,

Hanjin

## 8. Model Answer

Dear Julia,

I hope you are well.

I am writing to apologize for losing the book "Introduction to Algorithms" that I borrowed from you recently.

I kept the book on my bookshelf, but it appears to have gone missing. Despite searching thoroughly, I have been unable to locate it.

I sincerely apologize for this mistake. To make up for the loss, I will purchase a new copy and deliver it to you as soon as possible.

Thank you for your patience and understanding.

Best regards,

Hanjin

## 9. Useful Sentence Patterns

1. I am writing to apologize for...
2. Unfortunately, I have to admit that...
3. To make up for this, I will...
