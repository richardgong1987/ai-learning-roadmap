## 1. Original Question

Read the passage about how cities are trying to reduce traffic congestion. Write one sentence summarizing the main idea.

### Passage

Many cities are trying to reduce traffic congestion, but building more roads is no longer seen as a complete solution. In the past, governments often responded to crowded streets by widening roads or constructing new highways. This approach could provide short-term relief, but it also encouraged more people to drive, which eventually created the same problem again. As a result, urban planners are now looking for ways to manage demand rather than simply increase road capacity.

One common strategy is to improve public transport so that buses, trains, and trams become faster, cleaner, and more reliable. When people believe public transport will arrive on time and take them close to where they need to go, they are more likely to leave their cars at home. Some cities have also introduced dedicated bus lanes, integrated ticketing systems, and real-time travel information to make public transport easier to use. These changes may seem small, but together they can make daily travel less stressful and more predictable.

Another approach is to redesign streets for walking and cycling. Wider pavements, protected bike lanes, safer crossings, and lower speed limits can encourage residents to choose active forms of travel for short journeys. This does not mean that cars disappear completely. Instead, the goal is to give people more realistic choices, especially for trips that do not require a private vehicle. In some areas, local businesses initially worry that reducing car access will reduce customers, but studies of pedestrian-friendly streets often show that people spend more time in these areas when they feel safe and comfortable.

Technology can also help cities manage traffic more efficiently. Smart traffic lights can adjust to real-time conditions, parking apps can reduce the time drivers spend searching for spaces, and data from transport systems can help officials identify problem areas. However, technology alone cannot solve congestion if most people still depend on cars for every journey. For this reason, successful traffic management usually combines better public transport, safer street design, and smarter use of data.

Overall, the main challenge is not simply moving more cars through the city. It is creating a transport system where people can choose different ways to travel according to distance, cost, time, and convenience.

## 6. Mistake Analysis

### Content Problems

The sentence says cities reduce congestion by "building more roads". However, the passage says building more roads is not a complete solution and can encourage more driving. This changes the meaning.

The stronger main idea is: cities need to give people more travel choices instead of only moving more cars.

### Form Problems

The answer is one sentence and within the word limit. This part is good.

### Grammar Problems

Bad: "by building more roads, improving public transport, and redesigning streets..., and even leverage technology"

Good: "by improving public transport, redesigning streets..., and using technology"

The verbs after "by" should follow the same pattern: improving, redesigning, using.

### Vocabulary Problems

Bad: "improve management efficiently"

Good: "improve traffic management" or "manage traffic more efficiently"
