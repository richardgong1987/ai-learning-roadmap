## 1. Original Question

Read the passage about companies introducing artificial intelligence tools. Write one sentence summarizing the main idea.

### Passage

Many companies are investing in artificial intelligence tools to improve productivity, but the results are not always as simple as managers expect. In some workplaces, AI systems can complete routine tasks quickly, such as sorting customer messages, checking documents for errors, preparing simple reports, or helping employees find information faster. These tools can reduce repetitive work and allow staff to spend more time on tasks that require judgment, creativity, or personal communication. For this reason, business leaders often describe AI as a way to make employees more efficient rather than simply replace them.

However, introducing AI into the workplace also creates new challenges. Employees may feel worried if they do not understand how the technology works or if they believe their jobs are becoming less secure. Some workers may also become too dependent on AI-generated answers, especially when they are under time pressure. If they accept suggestions without checking them carefully, small mistakes can spread into reports, customer replies, or business decisions. This is especially risky in fields such as finance, healthcare, law, and education, where accuracy and responsibility are very important.

Training is therefore a key part of successful AI adoption. Companies that only buy new software but do not train their employees often fail to get the full benefit from the technology. Workers need to learn not only which buttons to press, but also when to trust AI, when to question it, and how to combine machine suggestions with human experience. Managers also need to explain clearly why AI is being introduced and how it will change daily work. Without this communication, employees may resist the system or use it in shallow ways.

Another important issue is fairness. AI tools are often trained on large amounts of past data, and this data may contain old patterns or hidden bias. If companies use AI to screen job applications, evaluate performance, or make customer decisions, they need to check whether the system treats people fairly. This means that AI adoption is not only a technical project, but also an ethical and organizational one.

Overall, AI can help companies work faster and more effectively, but only when it is introduced with proper training, careful checking, clear communication, and human responsibility.

## 2. Exam Instructions

Write one sentence only. The sentence should be 5-75 words. Compress several ideas into one clear sentence. Avoid listing too many details. Use correct grammar connectors, such as because, although, while, by, or so.

## 6. Mistake Analysis

### Content Problems

The answer correctly says AI improves productivity and creates challenges. However, it says companies should provide "proper training" only. The passage also says companies need careful checking, clear communication, fairness, and human responsibility.

### Form Problems

The answer is one sentence and within the word limit. This is good.

### Grammar Problems

Bad: "introduce AI for improving productivity"

Good: "introduce AI to improve productivity"

Bad: "it has also introduced many new challenges"

Good: "it also creates many new challenges"

The word "it" is a little unclear because the subject is "many companies". It is better to write "AI adoption" or "this technology".

### Vocabulary Problems

"Many new challenges" is understandable but general. A stronger phrase is "practical and ethical challenges".

"Rather than simply introducing AI" is good, but it should be connected to a fuller solution: "rather than simply buying new tools".
