## 6. Mistake Analysis

### Content Problems

The email says "one day off", but the dates cover two days: 2026-06-29 to 2026-06-30. The time period should be consistent.

### Form Problems

The closing is good, but punctuation can be improved. Add commas after greetings and closings where appropriate.

### Grammar Problems

- "I am writing to take one day off" should be "I am writing to request one day of leave".
- "At my leaving period" should be "During my absence" or "While I am away".
- "Yang-san would take over my task on my hand" should be "Yang-san will take over my tasks".
- "if you have any questions need to consult" should be "If you have any questions, please contact him".

### Vocabulary Problems

- Use "request leave" instead of "take one day off" in formal email.
- Use "During my absence" for professional handover.
- Use "I would appreciate it if you could approve my request" for a polite approval request.

## 7. Corrected Version

Hi Julia,
