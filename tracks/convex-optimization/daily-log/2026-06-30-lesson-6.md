Track: Convex Optimization

Completed lesson: Lesson 6 - Operations That Preserve Convexity

Summary:

The lesson covered operations that preserve convexity. The main idea is that new convex sets can be built from known convex sets without proving convexity from scratch every time.

Main operations:

- Intersection
- Affine transformation
- Projection
- Inverse image under affine mapping

Key example:

If A = [0, 10] and B = [4, 8], then A intersect B = [4, 8]. Since A and B are convex, their intersection is also convex.

Main intuition:

Convexity can be preserved when we combine convex sets in safe ways, especially by intersecting constraints or applying affine transformations.
