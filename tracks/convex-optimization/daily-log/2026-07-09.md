Lesson 10: Convex Optimization Problems

## What was learned

- An optimization problem has an objective function and constraints.
- A convex optimization problem requires a convex objective function.
- Inequality constraints must be convex functions in standard form fi(x) <= 0.
- Equality constraints must be affine, usually written as Ax = b.
- Feasible points satisfy all constraints.
- The optimal solution is the feasible point that gives the best objective value.

## Concrete examples used

- Laptop price minimization under a budget constraint.
- minimize x^2 subject to x >= 1.
- minimize x^2 subject to x >= 2.
- Rewriting x >= 2 as 2 - x <= 0.
- Comparing affine equality x + y = 5 with nonlinear equality x^2 + y = 5.

## Practical takeaway

To recognize a convex optimization problem, check three things: the objective must be convex, all inequality constraints must be convex, and all equality constraints must be affine.
